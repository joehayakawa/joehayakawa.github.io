May 23
Almaty, Kazakhstan
Arrival · Green Bazaar · Panfilov Park

Land at midnight, sleep, then explore Kazakhstan's largest city. The Green Bazaar is one of Central Asia's great markets — entire floors of dried fruits, nuts, spices. The wooden Russian Orthodox church in Panfilov Park was built without a single nail in 1907 and survived a 7.7 magnitude earthquake.

May 24–25
Bishkek & Ala Archa
Capital city · Glacier valley hike (16km, moderate)

Transit through Bishkek, then hit Ala Archa National Park — a dramatic gorge in the Tian Shan range with glacier-fed rivers, ancient fir forests, and snow-capped peaks above 4,000m. Your warm-up hike to the Ak-Sai glacier viewpoint (800m elevation gain, 4-5 hours).

May 26
Song-Kul Plateau ⚠️
3,016m · Nomadic yurt camps · Horse riding · Road may be closed

This is the Kyrgyzstan you came to see. A vast alpine lake encircled by open steppe — no trees, no fences, just grass, sky, horses, and yurts. Nomadic families bring their livestock here for summer. Sleep in a yurt, drink fermented mare's milk around a dung fire, and watch the Milky Way with zero light pollution.

May 27
Bokonbaevo & Issyk-Kul
Eagle hunting demo · World's 2nd largest alpine lake

The centre of Kyrgyzstan's living eagle hunting tradition (possibly 6,000 years old). A community-run demo where you see a golden eagle hunt a lure and sit on the hunter's arm. UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. Then sunset on the south shore of Issyk-Kul — 182km long, never freezes despite 1,600m altitude.

May 28
Karakol
Trekking capital · Dungan mosque · Russian church · Market town

Your hiking base camp. A city of 75,000 with a Chinese-style mosque built by Muslim Dungans who fled China in the 1870s, and a Russian Orthodox church built entirely from timber without nails — the whole messy history of Central Asia told in two buildings. Easy afternoon hike through Karakol gorge to stretch your legs.

May 29
Jyrgalan Valley
Echki-Jailoo loop (18km) · Ethical horse riding · Off the beaten path

A former Soviet coal-mining village transformed by community-based tourism. The Echki-Jailoo loop climbs through forest and shepherd camps to alpine meadows carpeted with wildflowers — yellow, purple, white. Marmots everywhere. The best place for ethical horse riding, with horses owned and guided by local families.

May 30
Altyn Arashan ✨
Hot springs · Glacier views · Yurt camp at 2,900m · NON-NEGOTIABLE

"Golden Spa" — a narrow river valley climbing from pine forest into alpine meadows, ending at natural hot springs with views to 4,000m+ glaciated peaks. Soak in naturally heated water as night falls and snow-capped mountains reflect in the pool. The water is mildly radioactive, "considered therapeutic in Central Asian tradition." Stars at altitude are extraordinary.
